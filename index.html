CSS Virginia USS Monitor

Alex, Caleb and I are presenting separately, to cover more about ironclads. Below are pictures of the USS Monitor and CSS Virginia with captions. An ironclad is a ship coated in metal, usually for protection. This metal coat protect the crew, and turned the iron clad into a wepon. Now the ironclad can ram a wooden ship, without risking damaging itself. Ironclads are the predictors of our modern day warships.

This is the USS Monitor. The Monitor had a crew of about 49 men. It had 2 cannons, positioned on a rotating turret.

This is the CSS Virginia. The Virginia had a crew of about 250 men. It had 12 cannons, spread on all sides of the boat. This design for the cannons was widely used in wooden boats.
